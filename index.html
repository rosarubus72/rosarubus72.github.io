PosterAgent: Academic Poster Generation from Papers

Yibin Yan, Weidi Xie
School of Artificial Intelligence, Shanghai Jiao Tong University
EMNLP 2024 Findings
Paper Code

Introduction

The task of generating academic posters from long scientific papers is a critical yet challenging endeavor in scientific communication. This paper introduces the PosterAgent framework, a multi-agent pipeline designed to address the complexities of this task. It leverages structured parsing, hierarchical planning, and visual feedback to generate high-quality posters. The main contributions include introducing a new benchmark, Paper2Poster, and proposing a 'Top-down' design philosophy that ensures global restructuring and local refinements.

How It Works

The PosterAgent framework operates through three key stages:

  1. Parser: Extracts key textual and visual content from the paper to build an asset library.
  2. Planner: Aligns assets and arranges them into coherent layouts, generating panels iteratively with a zoom-in mechanism.
  3. Painter-Commenter: Produces panel-level bullet points and ensures layout coherence and avoids overflow using a VLM as Commenter.
This top-down, visual-in-the-loop design ensures concise and coherent posters without manual tuning.

Experimental Results

Experimental results show that PosterAgent variants perform similarly in terms of overall robustness. The fully open-source stack (Qwen-Qwen) achieves a score of 114.65, closely following the best closed-source variant. Substituting Qwen for the VLM in the LLM-VLM configuration improves the PaperQuiz score, suggesting better balance between informativeness and visual appeal. The density-augmented scores also show improvements when Qwen is used as the VLM, enhancing aesthetic and overall quality.
