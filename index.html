PosterAgent: Auto-generate High-Quality Academic Posters

Yibin Yan, Weidi Xie
School of Artificial Intelligence, Shanghai Jiao Tong University
EMNLP 2024 Findings
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

The Paper2Poster project introduces PosterAgent, a multi-agent pipeline designed to automatically generate high-quality academic posters from scientific papers. It addresses the challenges of long-context and layout-sensitive tasks, leveraging structured parsing, hierarchical planning, and visual feedback. The main contributions include:

  1. Introducing a novel benchmark, Paper2Poster, for evaluating poster generation systems.
  2. Proposing a 'Top-down' design philosophy that involves global restructuring and local refinements.
  3. Developing PosterAgent variants, such as PosterAgent-4o and PosterAgent-Qwen, which show significant improvements in visual quality, text coherence, and overall aesthetic scores compared to existing methods.

Key Contributions

How It Works

PosterAgent follows a 'Top-down' design philosophy, which involves global restructuring and local refinements. The pipeline consists of three key components:

  1. Parser: Extracts key textual and visual content by tools and LLM-based summarization to build an asset library.
  2. Planner: Aligns assets and arranges them into coherent layouts, generating panels iteratively with a zoom-in mechanism.
  3. Painter-Commenter: The Painter produces panel-level bullet points and executable code for rendering, while a VLM as Commenter ensures layout coherence and avoids overflow.
