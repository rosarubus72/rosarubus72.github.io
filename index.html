PosterAgent: Academic Poster Generation Framework

What is it?

The paper proposes the PosterAgent framework, a multi-agent pipeline designed to generate high-quality posters from academic papers. It addresses the challenges of long-context and layout-sensitive tasks, leveraging structured parsing, hierarchical planning, and visual feedback.

Key Contributions:

  • A. Introducing a novel benchmark, Paper2Poster, for evaluating poster generation systems.
  • B. Proposing a 'Top-down' design philosophy that involves global restructuring and local refinements.
  • C. Developing PosterAgent variants, such as PosterAgent-4o and PosterAgent-Qwen, which show significant improvements in visual quality, text coherence, and overall aesthetic scores.

Why?

The research addresses the challenge of generating academic posters from long scientific papers that contain interleaved text, figures, and tables. The motivation stems from the need to efficiently communicate key findings at conferences where attendees have limited time.

Motivation:

  • A. To develop a benchmark and evaluation metrics for poster generation, focusing on visual quality, textual coherence, and the ability to convey core paper content.
  • B. To introduce PosterAgent, a multi-agent system that uses visual feedback to produce concise and coherent posters, addressing issues such as text overflow and spatial alignment.

How to do it?

Key Technologies:

  • A. LLM for semantic alignment of visual assets with textual content.

Implementation Steps:

  • B. Iteratively configuring the layout section by section.

How Effective is it?

The experimental results indicate that the PosterAgent variants perform similarly in terms of overall robustness, with PaperQuiz scores ranging from 114.09 to 118.25. The fully open-source stack (Qwen-Qwen) achieves a score of 114.65, closely following the best closed-source variant.
