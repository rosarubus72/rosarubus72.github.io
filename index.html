PosterAgent Framework: Generating High-Quality Academic Posters

Introduction

The paper introduces the PosterAgent framework, a multi-agent pipeline designed to generate high-quality posters from academic papers. It addresses the challenges of long-context and layout-sensitive tasks.

Main Contributions

  • Introducing a novel benchmark, Paper2Poster, for evaluating poster generation systems.
  • Proposing a 'Top-down' design philosophy involving global restructuring and local refinements.
  • Developing PosterAgent variants, such as PosterAgent-4o and PosterAgent-Qwen, which show significant improvements in visual quality, text coherence, and overall aesthetic scores.

Key Technologies

The framework leverages structured parsing, hierarchical planning, and visual feedback. Specifically:

  • LLM for semantic alignment of visual assets with textual content
  • Binary-tree layout strategy for panel bounding boxes
  • Human-AI collaboration for iterative refinement
  • Sequential execution of panel refinements

Experimental Results

The experimental results indicate that the PosterAgent variants perform similarly in terms of overall robustness, with PaperQuiz scores ranging from 114.09 to 118.25. The fully open-source stack (Qwen-Qwen) achieves a score of 114.65, closely following the best closed-source variant.

Implementation Steps

  1. Collecting visual and text assets
  2. Iteratively configuring the layout section by section
  3. Populating each panel with content
  4. Generating the entire poster in one shot (not recommended)

Conclusion

PosterAgent not only narrows the performance gap with human-designed posters but also establishes a new efficiency standard, offering a practical and scalable approach to scientific communication.
